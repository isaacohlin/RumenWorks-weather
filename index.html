Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD)

Neutral DMI ≈ 0°C
A neutral IOD has limited direct impact. A Positive IOD (warm western Indian Ocean) suppresses eastern Australian rainfall and typically co-occurs with El Niño — a compound drought driver.
⚠ Watch: Several climate models suggest possible positive IOD development mid-to-late 2026, which combined with El Niño would significantly compound drought risk for NSW.
Typical MNC impact of positive IOD: Dry May–Nov, reduced spring rainfall, earlier fire season onset.
